## preface

i've recently finished reading project hail mary by andy weir, and i wanted to jot down some of my thoughts on it to look back on later. its not exactly a review, but more of a mean to collect my raw thoughts on the book. that being said, i am unfortunately not too great at english or articulating my thoughts, so this might be a little messy.

## project hail mary

project hail mary is probably one of the best sci-fi/space-themed books i have read. the story involves ryland grace ("grace"), a middle school science teacher who wakes up from a coma with amnesia on a spaceship. he is the sole survivor, with two dead crewmates. earth's survival relies on grace, yet he doesn't know that. hell, he doesn't even know who he is.

the amnesia trope works perfectly (imo, though its not everyone's cup of tea) here, letting the reader unravel the situation alongside grace. as he slowly regained back his memory, he realised that he is humanity's last hope to stop an extinction-level event. 

this perspective is quite refreshing. oftentimes, stories are dominated by heavy, adverserial human conflicts (i.e., man vs man). however in project hail mary, the antagonist is a microscopic organism ("astrophage") + the unforgiving laws of physics. by stripping away human malice, the book carries a narrative where the driving force is sheer survival and cooperation. 

and there is where the book's highlight comes in. 

"rocky" is a highly intelligent alien lad that grace met along his journey. they learnt to communicate from scratch through musical notes, and sharing each others' culture from their own home planets (like watching each other sleep). their dynamic is THE heart and soul of the story. watching them use science to logic their way out of impossible dangers was genuinely fun.

weir manages to explain the heavy and technical science concepts without sounding condescending. given grace's background as well, his inner monologue is naturally educational but never patronising. he explains astrophysics, chemistry, molecular biology, etc., with desperate and enthusiastic bursts of energy. he explains just enough for the reader to experience the eureka moment right alongside rocky and grace.

ultimately, this hard sci-fi book is rather fast-paced and a deeply optimistic read. while i found certain parts a little draggy and the ending rather bittersweet, i enjoyed this book a lot! the movie will be out on 20 mar and im rather stoked to catch it soon.
